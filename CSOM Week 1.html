The Overall View

Coding is used in everything we do and every thing we uses.

There are many basic kinds of coding. Binary and HTML are some examples. But there is another kind of coding too. It’s called encryption. Encryption is when you take a message and you scramble it. There is always a key to any kind of encrypted code. One of the first people to use encryption was Julius Caesar.


The Caesar Cipher

The Caesar Cipher was first used (shockingly) by Julius Caesar. He used it to transmit messages in war.

The idea of the Caesar Cipher is very basic. There are twenty-six letters in the alphabet. You can move each of the letters over a maximum of twenty-five times. For example: A-E B-F C-A D-B E-C F-D. This would be if you moved each letter over four times to the right. Which brings me to another thing you alway move to the right. For Julius Caesar this was a huge step, and it was. But the Germans took it farther with Enigma.


The Father of Computers

There is one man that most people think of as the ‘father of computers’. That man is Alan Turing.

He was one of the many people charged with cracking the German codes that changed every twenty-four hours. To crack this code Turing invented a computer that could process any German code. This computer was called the Enigma Machine.
